Milady Maker is a collection of 9,999 unique NFTs developed by a generative algorithm. Each Milady is an entirely unique digital painting composed of traits in a novel generative algorithm designed to instill aesthetic consistency.

Milady's are designed to be profile-picture ready and double as entry tickets into the exclusive Milady Village community, and will also provide exclusive minting rights to a generative NFT Cover Photo.

Milady's have speculative value based on a complex of metrics: objective rarity, drip grade, core consistency and costume completion.

Fair Distribution

Milady Maker is fairly launched in a one-time drop at a minting fee of 0.1 Ethereum.

Biotrait Generation

Milady's base "biotraits" are randomly generated to create unique race, face & hair combinations. All biotraits maintain a standard distribution except for a selection of rarities and ultrararities.

Drip Generation

Milady's fashion accessories fall under three categories, Core, Costume and Prop:

  • A Core is a general sartorial theme with a large collection of items. Every core has its own rarity, and then each item has its own rarity within it. Every Milady can be rated on how much its accessories fall under the same aesthetic Core Consistency..
  • A Costume are rare halloween-style costume. Every costume has exactly one item for each of the major slots; a Milady that contains all the pieces of a costume is considered Costume Complete.
  • A prop are extra-rare one-off items that don't fit in any core or costume. These items do not count for or against Core Consistency or Costume Completion, but add value by being very rare.

Chain Generation

Milady Maker has a novel addition to its RNG algorithm designed to organically produce aesthetic consistency. As accessories are iteratively generated according to certain categories and sub-categories of various rarity levels, when one acessory is completed there is a RNG roll for a chance that the next one will fall under the same category or sub-category. This tips the finger on the scale to make high Core Consistency and high Costume Completion rated Milady's more frequent.

Metrics

Beyond simple objective rarity, every Milady can be valuated from a few set of novel angles introduced by the generative algorithm:

Drip Grade
Every accessory falls under 3 basic grades defined by their distribution rarity:

Basic Common
Drip Rare
Superdrip Ultrarare

This serves as a rough rule of thumb to catalogue Milady's into three buckets of overall accessory rarity.

Core Consistency
A Milady can also be rated by how much their total accessorization falls under a specific Core

Costume Completion
Every costume has a certain amount of items that complete it. A Milady can be scored by how close they come to completing any costume, as well as by the individual rarity of each costume.

On the Design Process

Milady Sonora's Neochibi Style
The artist behind this project is Milady Sonora, who has shown works in galleries both physically and digitally through Remilia Collective. They've developed their distinctive take on the Chibi Anime style in a portraiture series over the years, attempting to invoke a calming, ethereal, white-hearted angelicism in a blend of Western and Japanese aesthetics.

Sartorialism
The team was interested in approaching the generative art space from an angle of sartorialism, by using the Milady as a template for fashion accessorization. The thematic "Cores" were chosen around a loose narrative of throwback Tokyo street fashion, as seemed suited to Milady's fusion "neochibi" aesthetic. The Chain Algorithm was designed to lend the generated works towards more aesthetic coherency, something that seemed missing in earlier generative NFT projects which followed more traditional RNG.

Intuitive Value: Rarity <-> Drip
A major motivating factor for this design was also the desire for relative value to be intuitive; without knowing the 'meta' of the Milady market, one can ascertain higher or lower value Milady's at a glance by appraising their drip. This is achieved understanding the direct relationship between rarity and secondary market value; distribution rates were decided by first assessing each accessory by its "drip", making the highest drip accessories most rare and thus most valuable.

Profile-first
An interesting, unplanned development in generative NFT projects is buyers using the works as profile pictures on online communities. In retrospect, this makes sense, as the field operates almost entirely by word-of-mouth from digital networks. Unfortunately this is not something that most of the earlier generative NFT projects have designed with in mind. Mainly, they are too far zoomed out and have minimal background generation. While a user can manually crop their NFT and create a transparency to add a background, most do not; maybe out of lack of knowhow, or because its a violation of their NFT art - like drawing over a painting.

Milady's were designed to be great profile pictures from the start, with a higher vertical ratio so that a square crop will be a perfect zoom to convey emotional expressiveness, while still retaining the space to show off accessories. The design of the accessories also intentionally lent high-value rarities to larger or more distinctly colored pieces, with the intention that a valuable Milady can be read as such even as a thumbnail.

This is also why an optional NFT header add-in is planned as the gen 2 production, to cement our recognition of the unique relationship between generative NFT's and online spaces - perhaps as an early, organic structure for Ethereum based identities!
